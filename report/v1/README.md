## What the report claims

The writeup is anchored on the **v1 step-12 evaluation** ($n_b{=}30$ random
vs. $n_t{=}10$ trained) for all quantitative claims (Table 2, headline
numbers, training dynamics figures). The **extended-training corroboration
subsection (§5.5)** adds a separate 4000-step Kaggle run as supporting
evidence — its per-step infected-detection trajectory is the clearest
direct evidence of the hidden-role theory-of-mind signal the env was
designed to elicit, and the report explicitly notes that the Hugging Face
Space serves the extended-run adapter rather than the step-12 v1 adapter.

The report does not over-claim:

1. **OpenEnv compliance** — env passed all four R1 validator traps first try.
2. **100% format learning** — 0 parse failures across the trained eval.
3. **Reward-direction signal is unambiguous** — mean total reward 1.7×
   baseline, mean episode length 2.0× baseline.
4. **Emergent ToM broadcast** — the "I notice A2 is very hungry" quote.
5. **Cross-machine training resilience** — `hub_strategy="every_save"` made
   Colab/Kaggle disconnects cost <20 min of compute.
6. **Honest limitations** (§6.2) — single-step reward hook, sample size,
   single map, behavioural-cue leakage.

writeup (judges have to read several of these).

If asked to trim: §5.4 Behavioural Analysis can collapse to a footnote, and
